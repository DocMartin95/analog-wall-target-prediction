Which orphans stay effective after resistance mutations?

For each pathogen target we docked below-wall natural-product orphans plus the front-line drug into the wild-type pocket, then scored the binding-affinity change (ΔΔG) across the target's known pocket mutations. An orphan is flagged resistance-robust when its worst-case ΔΔG is at or below the drug's, i.e. it loses less affinity to resistance than the drug does. Lower ΔΔG = more robust.

A microbial-carotenoid cluster converging on PXR

Nine structurally related microbial carotenoids all nominated the xenobiotic-sensing receptor PXR (regulator of host CYP3A4). We docked them and the canonical PXR agonist rifampicin into the human PXR ligand-binding domain (PDB 1NRL). Every carotenoid lands within 1 kcal/mol of the rifampicin control, so the nomination holds at the structural level. Bars = docking affinity (more negative = stronger).
